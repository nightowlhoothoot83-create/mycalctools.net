About the Water Intake Calculator

This calculator estimates your recommended daily water intake based on your body weight, activity level, and the climate you live in. Adequate hydration is essential for regulating body temperature, supporting kidney function, maintaining concentration, and carrying nutrients to cells.

How to Use It

  1. Enter your body weight in kilograms (or pounds, if you prefer).
  2. Select your typical activity level — sedentary, lightly active, moderately active, or very active.
  3. Indicate whether you live in a hot climate (important for Australians in summer).
  4. The calculator returns your recommended daily water intake in litres and cups.

Hydration Tips for Australians

The Australian Dietary Guidelines recommend that adult men drink 2.6 litres of water per day and adult women 2.1 litres — but these figures are for a temperate climate with moderate activity. In the Australian summer, especially in inland or northern areas, needs can be significantly higher. A useful rule of thumb is to drink roughly 35 ml per kilogram of body weight per day at rest, adding 500 ml to 1 litre for every hour of exercise. Urine colour is a practical hydration indicator: pale straw-yellow means well-hydrated; dark yellow or amber means you need to drink more. Note that some medications and vitamins (especially B vitamins) can darken urine regardless of hydration status.
